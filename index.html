The Training Parachute

Athletes use training parachutes; small canopies that attach to the body and create resistance while running. They build strength. But you're meant to take them off.

Almost every leader is running with one they don't know is there.

Early in life, you learned an invisible pattern, a way of responding to pressure that helped you perform, achieve, and succeed. It worked. But it's still attached, quietly creating drag on everything you do. You're expending more effort than the task requires, carrying more stress than the situation demands.

This isn't a flaw. It's a near-universal human condition. It usually becomes visible at the highest levels of performance, where even small amounts of unnecessary drag make a meaningful difference.

Inner Success doesn't teach you to run harder. It cuts the parachute.

The Science Behind It

Inner Success draws on multiple disciplines to address the structural condition at its source — not its symptoms.

The programme integrates insights from neuroregulation, memory reconsolidation, interpersonal dynamics, and leadership theory. Each module is tailored to the individual, targeting the specific patterns that create their drag.

The underlying mechanism is well-understood: early experiences create responses that become automatic under pressure. These responses were adaptive once. In a leadership context, they show up as unnecessary reactivity, elevated stress, and effort that exceeds what the situation actually requires.

The programme doesn't manage these responses. It removes the triggers that activate them — permanently.

Eight Tailored Modules

Each programme is structured across eight modules delivered one-to-one. Before the programme begins, we measure the extent and pattern of the structural condition. After completion, we measure again.

This before-and-after data forms part of our growing evidence base — detailed in our research white paper.

The Origin

Tara Halliday was originally trained in conditional worth coaching — a discipline rooted in Carl Rogers' foundational psychology. Through her research, she discovered that conditional worth is the root cause of imposter syndrome.

Inner Success was built to address that root cause directly. The result was striking: imposter syndrome was eliminated as a byproduct — not because the programme targeted it, but because it removed the underlying condition driving it.

After years of one-to-one delivery, the far broader leadership impact became clear. The independent coach training programme then proved two things: the results are universal, and they don't depend on the originator.

Remove the interference first.
